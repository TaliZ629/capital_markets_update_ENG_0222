Why Markets Moved This Week

üìà Why Stocks Rose

Supreme Court Strikes Down Trump's Tariffs ‚Äî 6-3 Ruling

The dominant driver of this week's equity rally was a landmark Supreme Court ruling on Friday Feb 20 that President Trump's sweeping "Liberation Day" tariffs ‚Äî imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) ‚Äî were unconstitutional. The 6-3 decision ruled the White House exceeded its authority using emergency powers to impose broad import duties covering 100+ countries.

Stocks initially fell on disappointing Q4 GDP (+1.4% annualized vs. +2.5% expected) and sticky PCE inflation (+0.4% MoM), then reversed sharply after the midday ruling. Tariff-sensitive sectors led the rebound: Alphabet +3.7% Amazon +2.6% Etsy +8.4% Comm. Services +2.65% Cons. Discretionary +1.27%

For the full week: S&P 500 +1.07% Nasdaq +1.51% Dow +0.47%. Trump responded by announcing a new 10% global tariff under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 ‚Äî a different legal basis ‚Äî capping the rally's upside.

Sources: CNBC Markets ¬∑ Motley Fool ¬∑ NBC News ¬∑ Yahoo Finance
üíµ Why the Dollar Moved

Dollar Volatile: Initial Jump on Tariff Ruling, Then Reversed Lower

The U.S. dollar had a whipsaw session on Feb 20. The Supreme Court ruling initially caused a sharp dollar spike as investors priced in reduced trade deficits and lower import volumes ‚Äî less foreign currency demand for U.S. imports means less dollar selling. EUR/USD saw an immediate knee-jerk drop.

However, the dollar reversed lower by session end as markets digested that: (1) Trump immediately announced a replacement 10% global tariff, (2) Q4 GDP came in weak at just 1.4%, far below the 2.9% forecast, and (3) the Fed is in no rush to hike rates. The Dollar Index (DXY) closed near 97.67, down 0.2% on the day.

On the week, USD was broadly stronger vs. EUR (EUR/USD ‚àí0.83%) and JPY (USD/JPY +1.53%) as soft Eurozone data and BoJ uncertainty widened the rate differential in favor of the dollar.

Sources: U.S. News / Reuters ¬∑ Yahoo Finance / UBS ¬∑ Trading Economics
‚ö†Ô∏è Headwinds That Capped the Rally

Weak GDP, Sticky Inflation & Trump's 10% Replacement Tariff

Despite the tariff ruling tailwind, three factors prevented a larger rally: Q4 GDP grew just +1.4% annualized (est. +2.5%), dragged down by the 43-day government shutdown. Core PCE inflation came in at 3.0% YoY, reinforcing a "higher-for-longer" Fed narrative. FOMC minutes revealed internal disagreement ‚Äî some officials signaled rate hikes may still be needed if inflation persists.

Within hours of the SCOTUS ruling, Trump announced a replacement 10% global tariff under different legal authority (Section 122 / Trade Act of 1974), restoring uncertainty. Markets remain in a narrow 2026 trading range, with the S&P 500 up less than 1% YTD ‚Äî lagging the MSCI World index's +3%.

Week in Review

Interpretation

Cross-Asset Interpretation ¬∑ Week of Feb 20

The week's defining catalyst: the U.S. Supreme Court ruled 6‚Äì3 on Feb 20 that President Trump's sweeping IEEPA tariffs were unconstitutional, triggering an intraday reversal that lifted the S&P 500 +0.69% and Nasdaq +0.90% on the day alone. The relief rally reflected months of pent-up tariff anxiety unwinding ‚Äî Alphabet +3.7%, Amazon +2.6%, Etsy +8.4%, and consumer discretionary broadly outperformed. The full-week result: S&P 500 +1.07%, Nasdaq +1.51%, with VIX falling sharply ‚àí7.33% to 19.09.

The most striking divergence: crude oil surged +5.57% on U.S.‚ÄìIran nuclear tensions (Strait of Hormuz risk) while Bitcoin fell ‚àí2.58%, now down ‚àí22.29% YTD ‚Äî crypto decoupled from traditional risk-on assets entirely. Gold's relentless bid (+1.17% WoW, +17.46% YTD) alongside rising equities reflects hedging, not pure risk appetite ‚Äî investors are buying growth and insurance simultaneously, an unusual cross-asset signal.

The dollar whipsawed: it initially spiked on the tariff ruling (less trade = less dollar selling), then reversed lower as Trump announced a replacement 10% global tariff and Q4 GDP disappointed at just +1.4% annualized (vs. +2.5% expected). For the full week, USD strengthened vs. EUR (‚àí0.83%) and was mixed vs. JPY (+1.53%), driven by Europe's economic weakness and BoJ rate uncertainty. Credit spreads well-behaved (HY OAS ‚àí1.37%); 10Y yield at 4.086% signals the market still prices eventual Fed easing.
